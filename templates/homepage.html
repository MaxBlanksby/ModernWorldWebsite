Surveillance Artifacts - Modern World History

Overview

Surveillance has shaped the way societies function, from early theories of control to the modern digital world. This exhibit explores how the idea of being watched has influenced behavior, governance, and technology. Starting with early concepts like the Panopticon and Big Brother, we move through the rise of mass surveillance tools like CCTV and digital monitoring and finally examine how surveillance has become a part of daily life, from smart technology to entertainment. To accurately identify the forms of power within this curation, we will be using the Foucaldian modalities of power and Cultural Anthropology terms. By tracing this progression, we ask: how has constant observation changed the way we live?
